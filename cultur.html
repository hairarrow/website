Cultur: Your Event Calendar and Deal Finder

With this document, I want to present and discuss my insights and ideas for the Cultur service. This document should be seen as a living document until application development begins.

Goal

The most important item on this document. Every following item should be justified by describing how it helps our goals.

Help people discover events and deals they care about.

Approach

General guidelines that govern design decisions

Gather data from user interactions in the application to provide curated results. Design an approachable design aesthetic that generally stays out of the users way and highlights the content they want to see.

We subscribe to a clear design process for every component we want to create. This requires flexibility and abstract thought in regards to the unique components that will make up our application.

We establish and understand our key performance indicators to measure the success of a feature and the service as a whole. This will give us information that will help us make better design decisions.

User Objectives and Flow

The faster we learn what patterns are the most common, we can optimize that experience to be faster and delightful.

We need to define the interactions someone could have with our service and create clear expectations for them. Furthermore, we need to understand the basic patterns a user will take to receive the content they want.

Design Process

  1. Choose one component to work on
  2. Brain-storm ideas for that component
  3. Create sketches for those ideas; continue brain-storming
  4. After sketching, create a prototype of that component to test interactions.

  5. After finishing a prototype, we agree to stop iterating and start designing the next component on the agenda. We should still be flexible when new ideas come up, but heavy iteration should be avoided until we can make design decisions that are supported by UX research.

    This approach can be strict, but the goal is to minimize risk, reduce costs, and optimize the use of our resources.

  6. Present to the developers and ensure there's a clear understanding of the rationale, details and expectations of the design in a component.
  7. Review Component with developers and requests changes as needed.

La Carne

How we can use our approach to create major features that support our goals. These are the features I believe require the most attention and polish in order to create a beautiful, useful application that people will want to use frequently.

  1. Data Collection and Presentation Patterns

    backend, almost no visual design, expensive but worth it

    • Every action a user takes in the application should be tracked and analyzed to provide better content curation.
    • People need to subscribe to a category. We should optimize the events and deals displayed to correlate with a users interests.
    • Events and deals should be actionable. People should have the expectation that they can add an event to their calendars, share it with friends or groups of people, like an event or see similar events or deals, and dislike an event or deal. In the case of disliking an item, we should clarify the reason behind disliking to keep our curation engine accurate.
    • We should know what is trending in specific categories, events and collections.
    • Users should be able to follow other users, venues and artists. Events and deals from sources a person follows should have a higher importance in relation to other presentation components.

    • The Game

      I want to create a fun game to help us curate events and deals for our cultur users. This component would be included in different parts of our application and could have active and passive interactions.

      Interface Considerations: Passive

      As a crucial component in data acquisition, the game should be a persistent passive presence that sends out a signals that reward users when acting on an event or deal.

      This approach aims to create incentives for users to interact with our services and let them know we're actively curating the content they see. Think about Pandora's Music Streaming service, a big reason for their success in retention is how it remembers a users musical taste to curate the content presented.

      Interface Considerations: Active

      We would give users an option to quickly catch up on events or deals that could interest them with a card-swiping game.

      When we consider how to display information, we usually decide that an item should display relevant data points about the content; this includes addresses, pictures, prices, categories, people going, and many more. We find a problem when we present a list of many items that is easily cluttered depending on the amount of data displayed.

      A card interface facilitates the display of more relevant data because a user is only shown one element that allows for focused interactions. Furthermore, if they play the game, they're forced to take some sort of action on an event which will help our curation efforts.

  2. Notifications

    backend, native, intelligent, MUST

    Rule 1: Notifications need to be intelligent.

    Too many notifications will be annoying and could harm the user-base; too few notifications will be ignored in the slurry of signals a user receives in a day.

    Rule 2: Notifications should be curated

    We should only send notifications after establishing a strong correlation between the item and a user's interests.

    Rule 3: Notifications should be geo-fenced

    After all, that's how we're going to make money on deals and featured events, but these will always need to pass rule 1 and 2.

    Notifications should follow our rules and cover:

    • Upcoming events or deals
    • Nearby deals
    • Events coming up in a weekend
    • Featured events
    • New collections

  3. On-boarding

    pleasant, simple, useful, MUST

    Establish a simple method to establish a base line of results for a user.

    We present different functions of the service to establish clear expectations.

    This could use "the game" to kick everything off and familiarize people with the different functions of our service.

    Take a moment to justify and ask for permissions, while establishing expectations.

    • Notifications: We will only send notifications for items that interest that person. They will always have the power to tweak with their notification options to ensure they only receive content they care about.
    • Location: We will use location services to let people know when something interesting is happening near them, or if they could take advantage of a nearby deal the want.
    • Facebook: We use Facebook to help people share items with friends and see what deals or events their friends have share or expressed interest in. We will never post to Facebook without a users permission.

  4. Collections

    front-end, graphic design, back-end, machine-assisted editorial content

    Collections are curated lists of events and deals that match a specific theme. For example:

    • Reggae Concerts
    • Outdoors Family Activities
    • Technology meet-ups and Presentations
    • This Weekend
    • Most Popular for [some category]
    • Editor's Choice

    These collections could be presented as a list and could incorporate the active interface of "the game". They should be updated frequently and have constraints that dictate who sees them. Collections will be a part of a cultur, and as such, they could be featured at the top filtered results and will only be displayed to people that follow those culturs.

    Basically, they're collections of events and deals that belong to a specific cultur and have a shared theme.

  5. Search

    back-end, front-end, delightful

    We need a search that is capable of finding users, musicians, venues, events, and deals.

    The search should be flexible enough to answer these questions:

    • What events or deals of category are happening between {date range}?
    • What is friend doing this weekend?
    • I'm traveling to location, what's happening there?
    • What events or deals are happening at Venue?
    • What events or deals of any category are coming up at any venue?

  6. Dashboard

    front-end, UX

    The first thing that people see when they open the app should be curated modules of things they're interested in.

    We need to present it as a hub that can easily take them to the feature they want to use, but also present content that could be acted on quickly.

    Dashboard Components

    • Relevant collections
    • frequent searches
    • Upcoming events that fit user interests
    • Events or deals nearby (regardless of category)

  7. The following are components that will also be designed following our approach, but might require further discussion.

    • Featured Profiles for Artists and Venues
    • Automatic or custom groups of people with similar interests
    • Events/Deals List
    • Event/Deal Items
    • List of Culturs (Thumbnails could require graphic design)
    • Saved events list
    • "My Calendar" view to provide upcoming events a user has said they're going to or might be interested in
    • Settings and privacy considerations
    • Notifications within the application
    • Inviting people to events
    • Inviting people to the service
    • Live streaming for events
    • Event creation (which could be a featured saved for venues or artists)
    • Infrastructure considerations (moving to AWS, Facebook Live API)
    • Check-in
